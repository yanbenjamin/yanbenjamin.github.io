Fiction

During my childhood summers, my parents would always drop me off at the library. Because (1) they believed I should read and (2) I was so fucking annoying as a kid that getting rid of me from 9 AM to 5 PM became as much of a family staple as dumpling soup. So, I fell in love with reading. Next to dim lamps, I would excavate hours inside fictional worlds full of sorcery and witchcraft, where carptets could fly, candles would dance, and anything (I mean anything) could happen. I loved the library so much that I was always bummed to go home, and back to my ordinary Muggle life.

As a result, I also fell in love with writing—at least the idea of it. I was obsessed with my favorite writers and had a fever to become one someday. This was around when I met my writer's block, which pummeled my scrawny, idealistic ass with the force of a thousand suns.

Needless to say, I did not end up a devoted writer. Instead, I became a basic, boring, overly self-conscious cookie-cutter techie. But I still write stories here and there. Tiny fantasy tales, imaginings of the future, love epistolaries, and poems crossed with RhymeZone DNA.

To get to the point lol, at the moment, I'm too embarrassed by my current quality to share anything. But I hope to put some fiction work here in the future 🙃.
