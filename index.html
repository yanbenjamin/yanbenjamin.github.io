About Me

Hi! I am a M.S. student in Computer Science at Stanford University. I'm also pursuing my B.S. in Computer Science and Mathematics, plus a minor in Creative Writing. I co-teach Stanford CS 106S: Coding for Social Good, which is offered all quarters except summer. In 2020, I was named a U.S. Presidential Scholar.

My principal research area is deep learning for sustainability, especially in healthcare and climate change. Currently, I investigate methane sources in mosaics of global satellite imagery. I'm affiliated with Prof. Andrew Ng's Stanford ML Group, Prof. Jure Leskovec's SNAP Group, and Harvard Medical School as a visiting research fellow in medical AI, advised by Dr. Michael Moor and Prof. Pranav Rajpurkar. Previously, I worked at NVIDIA and the Stanford Natural Language Processing (NLP) Group.

In my free time, I evangelize Across the Spider-Verse and all kinds of animated masterworks (e.g. Gravity Falls, Arcane, Spirited Away, The Owl House), read short stories, and write poetry. My pronouns are he/they.
