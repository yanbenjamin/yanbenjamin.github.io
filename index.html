About Me

Hi! I am a M.S. student in Computer Science at Stanford University. I'm also pursuing my B.S. in Computer Science and Mathematics, plus a minor in Creative Writing. I co-teach Stanford CS 106S: Coding for Social Good, which is offered all quarters except summer. In 2020, I was named a U.S. Presidential Scholar. My research orbits around deep learning for planetary sustainability, along the axis of human health and climate change.

In my free time, I watch animation (e.g., Spider-Verse, Gravity Falls, Spirited Away, The Owl House), and read the fiction column of The New Yorker. My pronouns are he/they. I'm American-born, of Chinese heritage, and grew up in the forested chrysalis that is Minnesota.
